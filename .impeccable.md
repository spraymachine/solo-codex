## Design Context

### Users
- Mani and Harti both use this as a shared personal operating system.
- It supports day-to-day execution, reflection, and progress tracking across work, routines, and self-development.
- The primary jobs are ticking off tasks, seeing why each challenge matters, logging feelings, and turning personal development into a game-like system.

### Brand Personality
- Sharp
- Bold
- Extremely functional
- The interface should feel decisive, high-signal, and operational rather than soft, decorative, or lifestyle-oriented.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Mission control dashboard, not a journal app and not a generic productivity SaaS.
- The experience should feel synchronized, dense in meaning, and visually disciplined.
- Mani keeps the blue accent and Harti keeps the green accent, but the overall surface language should remain cohesive and controlled.
- The page should privilege task flow, context, and momentum over explanation or marketing-style framing.

### Design Principles
1. Show action first: the next thing to do should always be visually obvious.
2. Every task needs context: "why" should sit close to the task, not be buried in a modal or secondary page.
3. Group by operational meaning, not by feature bucket: related information should live together in compact control zones.
4. Gamification should reinforce discipline: progress, streaks, clears, and logs should feel earned, not playful noise.
5. Keep the interface sharp and quiet: fewer containers, tighter hierarchy, and stronger alignment over decorative clutter.
